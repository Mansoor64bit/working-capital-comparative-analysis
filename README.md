# Working Capital Management Comparative Analysis

## Overview

This project presents a comparative analysis of working capital management practices in the Information Technology (IT) and Automobile sectors.

The study evaluates how effectively companies manage short-term assets and liabilities by analyzing key working capital ratios over a five-year period from FY 2021 to FY 2025.

The analysis covers leading companies from both sectors and highlights industry-specific differences in inventory management, receivables management, and working capital utilization.

---

## Objectives

- Compare working capital efficiency within the IT sector.
- Compare working capital efficiency within the Automobile sector.
- Analyze differences between service-based and manufacturing industries.
- Evaluate working capital trends over a five-year period.
- Identify best practices and areas for improvement.

## Financial Ratios Used

### Working Capital Turnover Ratio

Measures how efficiently working capital is utilized to generate revenue.

### Inventory Turnover Ratio

Measures how efficiently inventory is managed and converted into sales.

### Debtors Turnover Ratio

Measures the effectiveness of receivables collection and credit management.

## Methodology

- Collection of secondary financial data
- Ratio calculation and analysis
- Trend analysis (FY 2021–FY 2025)
- Descriptive statistical analysis
- Sector-wise comparison
- Interpretation of findings

---

## Key Findings

### IT Sector

- Demonstrated consistent working capital efficiency.
- Maintained minimal inventory requirements.
- Showed stable receivables management.

### Automobile Sector

- Exhibited higher inventory turnover requirements.
- Demonstrated faster debtor collection cycles.
- Showed greater volatility in working capital utilization.

### Sector Comparison

- IT companies benefited from low inventory dependency.
- Automobile companies relied heavily on inventory and supply chain management.
- Business model differences significantly influenced working capital structure.
